About - Digital Ethics



WWhat is Digital Ethics?: Clinical & Ethical Considerations Digital Ethics is the study of how to manage oneself ethically, professionally and in a clinically sound manner via online and digital mediums. Every psychologist who uses the Internet or a cell phone (which amounts to nearly all of us) must look at issues, such as:

What can my clients find out about me online? Is it ethical for me to Google my clients? What if I find out information about my clients that is clinically significant? Should I be Facebook friends with my clients? How do I handle clients’ Friend requests on Facebook, or follows on Twitter?

The best way to explore these questions is to start doing research. Curious about what your clients can find out about you online? Conduct a search on yourself, using several different search terms, such as: "Dr. John Smith, Notown, State" or "Dr. Smith, psychologist" and so on. You may be surprised at what you find.

Be wise about Googling your clients. If you find something about them that is important, and they had not told you, this may alter the course of therapy. You may be in a position where your choices are to tell the client you Googled them (which they may or may not mind), or to try and separate what they tell you from what you found out elsewhere. Your clients may very well Google you! And they are not – nor should they be – restricted from doing so in any way.

A negative online review can be damaging for your career and/or personality.
